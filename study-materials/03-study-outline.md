# MET Exam — Study Outline

## How to Use This Outline

This outline organizes MET preparation into 10 study sessions. Each session covers one practice unit from the platform, progressing from B1+ to B2 level. Study one unit per session.

---

## Session 1: Hotel Calm Check-In (Unit 1, B1+)

### Theme: Accommodation & Arrival

**Reading Practice** (20 min)
- Read about hotel check-in scenarios
- Practice scanning for specific details
- Practice inference from context

**Grammar** (15 min)
- Present Simple vs Present Continuous
- Habitual routines → Present Simple
- Temporary situations → Present Continuous
- Adverbs of frequency (usually, often, always) → Present Simple
- Duration markers (this week, currently, today) → Present Continuous

**Speaking** (15 min)
- Describe a hotel check-in scene (60s, individual)
- Use: foreground, background, details, inference

**Writing** (15 min)
- Informal email about a short trip (80-120 words)
- Use past tenses, organize ideas clearly, include feeling/opinion

**Vocabulary** (10 min)
- reception, reservation, key card, elevator, lobby, available, included, quiet room

---

## Session 2: Perfect Weekend Apartment (Unit 2, B1+)

### Theme: Reviews & Recommendations

**Reading** (20 min)
- Read reviews and opinion texts
- Practice skimming for gist
- Recognize opinion language

**Grammar** (15 min)
- Past Simple vs Past Continuous
- Completed actions → Past Simple
- Background interrupted actions → Past Continuous
- Narrative sequencing

**Speaking** (15 min)
- Recommend a restaurant (pair work, 3 min role-play)
- Use recommendations and reasons

**Writing** (15 min)
- Restaurant review (100-140 words)
- Include description, opinion, recommendation

**Vocabulary** (10 min)
- whole-grain, vegetarian, dairy-free, smoothie, portion, balanced meal, fresh, ingredient

---

## Session 3: Mid-Mountain Health Retreat (Unit 3, B1+)

### Theme: Retreats & Reservations

**Reading** (20 min)
- Detailed comprehension of retreat information
- Vocabulary in context

**Grammar** (15 min)
- Present Perfect for experiences
- Life experiences with "ever/never"
- Duration with "for" and "since"
- Past experiences connected to present

**Speaking** (15 min)
- Develop a balanced weekend plan (90s individual presentation)

**Writing** (15 min)
- Travel itinerary draft (80-120 words)

**Vocabulary** (10 min)
- commute, delay, route, gate, connection, traffic, cancel, refund

### Theme: Airport Wellness & Delays

**Reading** (20 min)
- Scan for specific information
- Understand announcements and schedules

**Grammar** (15 min)

**Writing** (15 min)

**Vocabulary** (10 min)

**Vocabulary** (10 min)

### Theme: Community Walking & Transportation
