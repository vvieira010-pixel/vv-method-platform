# Listening

Scripts are ready for teacher recording or TTS generation. Each includes pre-, while-, and post-listening stages.

## listen_life_01 — Hotel Check-In with a Room Request
**Difficulty:** B1+
**Type:** conversation

**Script:**
Receptionist: Good evening. Welcome to River View Hotel. How can I help you? Guest: Hi, I have a reservation under Daniel Costa. Receptionist: Let me check. Yes, one single room for two nights. Guest: That’s right. I also requested a quiet room because I have an online meeting tomorrow morning. Receptionist: I see the note here. We can give you a room on the fifth floor, away from the elevator. Guest: That would be perfect. Is breakfast included? Receptionist: Yes, breakfast is served from seven to ten. The gym opens at six if you want to exercise before your meeting. Guest: Great. I’ll probably use it tomorrow. Receptionist: Here is your key card. Enjoy your stay.

**Pre-listening:** Ask: What do people usually request when checking in at a hotel?
**While-listening:**
- l1_q1: How long is Daniel staying?
- l1_q2: Why does he want a quiet room?
- l1_q3: What time does the gym open?
**Post-listening:** Role-play a hotel check-in and make one lifestyle-related request.

## listen_life_02 — Directions to a Healthy Café
**Difficulty:** B1+
**Type:** conversation

**Script:**
Tourist: Excuse me, is there a healthy café near here? Local: Yes, there’s one called Green Corner. It’s about ten minutes away on foot. Tourist: Great. How do I get there? Local: Walk straight along this street until you see the public library. Turn left there, and keep walking for two blocks. The café is next to a bike shop. Tourist: Is it expensive? Local: Not really. They have lunch bowls, smoothies, and soup. It’s popular with students and office workers, so it can get crowded after twelve thirty. Tourist: Maybe I’ll go now, then. Local: Good idea. If you arrive before noon, you’ll probably find a table.

**Pre-listening:** Students predict café vocabulary: smoothie, bowl, crowded, table.
**While-listening:**
- l2_q1: How far away is the café on foot?
- l2_q2: What is the café next to?
- l2_q3: When does it get crowded?
**Post-listening:** Students give directions from school/home to a healthy place nearby.

# Grammar

Ten grammar exercises with lifestyle/travel contexts, examples, and answer keys.

## gram_life_01 — Present Simple vs Present Continuous
**Difficulty:** B1+
**Focus:** Routines and temporary situations

**Examples:**
- I usually walk to work.
- This week I am staying at a hotel.

**Task:**
- 1) I usually ___ breakfast at home. (have)
- 2) This week, she ___ in a rented apartment. (stay)
- 3) They ___ yoga every Monday. (do)
- 4) We ___ for a quiet café right now. (look)
- 5) He often ___ his phone before bed. (check)

## gram_life_02 — Past Simple vs Past Continuous
**Difficulty:** B1+
**Focus:** Travel experiences

**Examples:**
- I was waiting at reception when my friend arrived.
- We missed the bus yesterday.

**Task:**
- 1) I ___ dinner when the hotel called. (have)
- 2) She ___ her suitcase at the airport. (lose)
- 3) We ___ to the market when it started raining. (walk)
- 4) They ___ a great café last weekend. (find)
- 5) He ___ when the announcement started. (sleep)

## gram_life_03 — Present Perfect for Experiences
**Difficulty:** B1-B2
**Focus:** Life and travel experiences

**Examples:**
- I have stayed in many hostels.
- She has never tried a digital detox.

**Task:**
- 1) I ___ never ___ a wellness retreat. (try)
- 2) She ___ already ___ the hotel. (book)
- 3) We ___ visited this market before. (not)
- 4) Have you ever ___ a flight? (miss)
- 5) They ___ just ___ from their trip. (return)

## gram_life_04 — Future Forms
**Difficulty:** B1-B2
**Focus:** Plans, predictions, and decisions

**Examples:**
- I’m going to visit the market.
- I’ll call the hotel now.

**Task:**
- 1) We ___ stay near the beach next month. (plan)
- 2) I think the café ___ be crowded. (prediction)
- 3) The train is late, so I ___ take a taxi. (decision now)
- 4) She ___ meeting the guide at 9. (arrangement)
- 5) They ___ probably enjoy the walking tour. (prediction)

## gram_life_05 — Modals for Advice and Suggestions
**Difficulty:** B1-B2
**Focus:** Lifestyle advice

**Examples:**
- You should drink more water.
- You could try walking after work.

**Task:**
- 1) You ___ book early because the hotel is popular.
- 2) You ___ try a digital detox if notifications stress you.
- 3) You ___ eat in the Quiet Zone; it is not allowed.
- 4) We ___ take the bus, but a taxi is faster.
- 5) You ___ talk to reception if the room is noisy.

## gram_life_06 — Comparatives and Superlatives
**Difficulty:** B1-B2
**Focus:** Comparing lifestyle options

**Examples:**
- This room is quieter than the first one.
- The earliest train is the most convenient.

**Task:**
- 1) A smaller city is usually ___ than a capital. (quiet)
- 2) This café is ___ than the one near the station. (healthy)
- 3) The gym is the ___ place in the hotel. (busy)
- 4) Walking is ___ expensive than taking taxis. (little)
- 5) This is the ___ routine I have tried. (good)

## gram_life_07 — First Conditional
**Difficulty:** B1-B2
**Focus:** Real future possibilities

**Examples:**
- If the train is late, we will take the bus.
- If you arrive early, you will find a table.

**Task:**
- 1) If the café ___ crowded, we ___ take food away. (be / get)
- 2) If you ___ before noon, you ___ a quiet table. (arrive / find)
- 3) If it ___ heavily, the tour ___ canceled. (rain / be)
- 4) If she ___ her notifications, she ___ calmer. (turn off / feel)
- 5) If we ___ late, we ___ the instructor. (be / message)

## gram_life_08 — Second Conditional
**Difficulty:** B2-
**Focus:** Hypothetical lifestyle choices

**Examples:**
- If I lived near the beach, I would walk every morning.
- If I had more time, I would cook more often.

**Task:**
- 1) If I ___ remotely, I ___ to a smaller city. (work / move)
- 2) If she ___ more free time, she ___ a walking group. (have / join)
- 3) If we ___ near a market, we ___ fresh food more often. (live / buy)
- 4) If he ___ fewer notifications, he ___ less stressed. (receive / feel)
- 5) If they ___ a car, they ___ weekend trips. (have / take)

## gram_life_09 — Relative Clauses
**Difficulty:** B2-
**Focus:** Describing places and people

**Examples:**
- The café that opened near my office is excellent.
- The guide, who was very friendly, explained the route.

**Task:**
- Combine: 1) The hotel is near the river. We stayed there.
- 2) The receptionist was helpful. She changed my room.
- 3) The app sends notifications. It helps me track habits.
- 4) The market opens on Sundays. It sells local food.
- 5) The walking group meets outside the library. It is free.

## gram_life_10 — Linking Words for B2 Answers
**Difficulty:** B2
**Focus:** Contrast, result, reason, addition

**Examples:**
- The city is expensive; however, it offers many opportunities.
- I turned off notifications because they distracted me.

**Task:**

- 2) I booked early ___ prices increase quickly.
- 3) The train was canceled; ___, we took the bus.
- 4) ___ the café was crowded, the service was fast.
- 5) The hotel has a gym. ___, it offers healthy breakfast options.

# General Teaching Notes
- Use the pack progressively: Reading/Listening 1-3 for B1+, 4-7 for B1-B2 transition, 8-10 for stronger B2 practice.
- For platform use, objective tasks can be auto-corrected. Speaking and writing should be teacher-reviewed or reviewed with a rubric.
- For MET-style development, train students to support answers with reason + example, especially in speaking and writing.
- Keep student-facing feedback concise: one strength, one focus area, and one next action.
