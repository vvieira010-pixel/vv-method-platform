# Part 1 — Extended Dialogue

## Workplace Technology: Inventory Management System

**Manager:** So, Maria, you've been working with the new inventory management system for a couple of weeks now. How are you finding it?

**Maria:** Well, to be perfectly honest, it has been a bit of a mixed bag so far. On the one hand, I can definitely see the potential benefits, especially when it comes to streamlining daily tasks and reducing the amount of manual work we used to do. The automated reports, for example, are a real improvement. They save time, reduce the risk of human error, and make it much easier to keep track of stock levels across different departments.

That being said, I must confess that the transition hasn’t been as smooth as I had hoped. The initial training was quite rushed, and we were given a lot of information in a very short period of time. As a result, I still feel like I’m fumbling around with some of the more advanced features, particularly when I need to customize reports or correct inventory discrepancies. Sometimes I know the system can probably do what I need, but I’m not always sure where to find the right function.

Another issue is that the constant system updates have been quite disruptive. You barely get used to one version, and then suddenly the layout changes or a feature is moved somewhere else. It can be frustrating, especially during busy periods when we don’t really have time to stop and figure out what has changed. A few colleagues have said the same thing, so I don’t think it’s just me.

Overall, I’d say the system is promising, but we need more support. If we had a follow-up training session, maybe with practical examples based on real problems we face every day, I think people would feel much more confident using it. At the moment, it’s efficient in theory, but in practice, we’re still adjusting.

# Part 10 — Platform Review Prompt

Use this prompt when you want AI to review your student platform.

## Complete Version

I am building a student platform for my Michigan English Test preparation students. Most of my students are adults, especially healthcare professionals and nurses preparing for the MET for professional or immigration purposes.

Please review the platform carefully from the perspective of:

1. **Student experience**
   - Is the platform clear and easy to understand?
   - Can students quickly know what they need to do next?
   - Is the layout too confusing, too empty, or too overloaded?

2. **MET preparation quality**
   - Does the platform support the main skills tested in the Michigan English Test: speaking, writing, listening, reading, grammar, and vocabulary?
   - Are the activities and feedback sections useful for MET preparation?
   - Does it feel focused on real exam progress?

3. **Teacher workflow**
   - Is it easy for the teacher to diagnose students, assign homework, review submissions, and send feedback?
   - Are there any parts that seem repetitive, unnecessary, or missing?
   - What could make the workflow faster and more practical?

4. **Dashboard and navigation**
   - Are the menus, sections, cards, and buttons clear?
   - Does the dashboard show the most important information first?
   - Are there any labels or section names that sound unnatural or unclear?

5. **Feedback and progress tracking**
   - Does the platform make student progress visible in a simple way?
   - Is the feedback area helpful without being too long or overwhelming?
   - Should anything be simplified, renamed, removed, or reorganized?

6. **Professional appearance**
   - Does the platform look serious, trustworthy, and appropriate for a premium English test preparation course?
   - Does it match the target audience of adult healthcare professionals?
   - What design improvements would make it look more polished?

Please give me a complete and honest review. Point out what is working well, what feels confusing, what may be missing, and what should be improved first. Be direct and practical. After the review, give me a prioritized action plan divided into:

- Urgent fixes
- Important improvements
- Nice-to-have features for later

## Short Version

Please review my Michigan English Test preparation platform from the perspective of a student and a teacher. I want honest feedback about the layout, navigation, dashboard, student experience, MET preparation quality, feedback system, homework flow, progress tracking, and overall professional appearance.

Tell me what is working, what is confusing, what is missing, and what should be improved first. Be direct, practical, and specific. At the end, give me a prioritized action plan with urgent fixes, important improvements, and features that can wait for later.

# Final Teacher Note

Do not give all vocabulary at once.

Recommended use:

- 20 words per homework topic
- 8–10 active words required in writing
- 5 active words required in speaking
- Review old vocabulary every two sessions
- Use the same vocabulary again in listening and reading exercises
- Correct students not only for meaning, but also for natural usage
- Keep the homework manageable and focused
