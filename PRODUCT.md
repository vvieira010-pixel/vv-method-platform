# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo English teacher (Vinicius) managing a roster of students preparing for the MET (Michigan English Test). Students access their own dashboard to view homework, complete practice exercises, track progress, and receive AI-powered feedback. The teacher diagnoses skill gaps, assigns targeted homework, reviews submissions, and monitors progress across the full teaching cycle: Class > Diagnosis > Feedback + Homework > Review.

## Product Purpose

A focused MET preparation platform that replaces scattered spreadsheets, PDFs, and messaging with a single integrated workflow. Success looks like: every student has a clear diagnosis, targeted practice, timely feedback, and visible progress toward their MET score target. The platform is the teacher's command center and the student's practice environment.

## Brand Personality

Calm, focused, expert. The interface should feel like working with a knowledgeable private tutor who has everything organized. Quiet confidence over flashy energy. Every screen should communicate competence and clarity.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: cookie-cutter admin panels with default blue buttons and endless data tables. This is a teaching tool, not enterprise software.
- **Overloaded school LMS** (Google Classroom, Moodle): cluttered, dated, institutional feel. Navigation should be intuitive, not bureaucratic.
- **Flashy edtech startups**: too many animations, mascots, gamification-for-its-own-sake. Gamification exists here to motivate, not to distract.

## Design Principles

1. **Diagnosis is the source of truth** — every student interaction traces back to their diagnosed skill gaps. The UI should make this lineage visible.
2. **Show progress, not complexity** — students should feel momentum. Teachers should see the full picture without drowning in data.
3. **Expert confidence** — the interface should feel like it was built by someone who understands language teaching, not a generic form builder.
4. **Quiet until needed** — features surface contextually. Empty states guide, errors explain, and nothing competes for attention.
5. **Keyboard and density welcome** — inspired by Linear: information-dense when useful, but never cluttered. Power users should feel fast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AAA target. Enhanced contrast ratios (7:1 for normal text, 4.5:1 for large text), full keyboard navigation, reduced-motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`, screen reader landmarks and ARIA labels, focus-visible indicators on all interactive elements. Color is never the sole indicator of state.
