# MET Proficiency Mastery — Project Instructions

This project is for **MET Proficiency Mastery**, a professional English test preparation platform focused mainly on nurses and healthcare professionals preparing for the Michigan English Test.

The platform should feel serious, clean, supportive, and premium — not childish, not overly colorful, and not generic.

The main goal is to help students prepare for the MET with clear diagnosis, targeted practice, useful feedback, and visible progress.

---

## Brand Identity

Use this brand direction consistently:

- Main color: navy blue
- Accent color: teal
- Supporting colors: soft mint, white, light gray
- Body text: slate gray
- Style: clean, professional, calm, mature, and easy to read
- Feeling: trustworthy, organized, supportive, and exam-focused

The platform should look appropriate for adult learners, especially nurses and healthcare professionals.

Avoid anything that feels too playful, childish, messy, or visually overwhelming.

---

## Product Positioning

The course/platform is:

**MET Proficiency Mastery**
**Michigan English Test Preparation for Nurses**
**Your pathway to MET confidence**

The focus is:

- 70% MET preparation
- 30% healthcare communication practice

The healthcare part should support the MET goal, not replace it.

## Main Users

The main students are adult English learners, especially nurses and healthcare professionals, who need English for:

- MET preparation
- professional goals
- immigration or work pathways
- healthcare communication
- confidence in real-life English situations

The teacher/admin user needs tools to create, review, and manage student learning materials efficiently.

---

## Student Experience

Student-facing language should be simple, natural, and encouraging.

Avoid long diagnostic explanations for students. They should not feel overwhelmed.

Student feedback should usually include:

- a short class summary
- one strong point
- one main improvement focus
- one clear next step

Do not create many repeated sections like "What you did well" and "What to improve" every time if it sounds unnatural.

Prefer warmer, more human section names, such as:

- Today's focus
- What is becoming stronger
- Your next step
- Practice focus
- Keep working on this

The student should always understand:

1. What they practiced
2. What improved
3. What they should focus on next

---

## Teacher Experience

The teacher needs more detailed diagnostic information, but this should stay teacher-only unless intentionally shared.

Teacher-facing notes may include:

- diagnostic observations
- skill weaknesses
- examples of student errors
- recommended next class
- homework suggestions
- vocabulary needs
- grammar focus
- readiness concerns

The teacher should always have a chance to preview, edit, and approve feedback before it is sent or saved for the student.

Do not automatically publish sensitive or detailed diagnostic notes to students.

---

## Scoring and Progress Rules

Do not assign scores to skills that were not evaluated.

If a skill was not practiced enough, show something like:

- Not evaluated yet
- Not enough evidence
- 0 turns evaluated
- Needs more samples

Do not estimate scores just to fill the dashboard.

The platform should be honest about what has and has not been evaluated.

Target score selection should happen before diagnosis when possible.

Important target profiles include:

- Endorsement goal: 55 overall / 55 speaking
- VisaScreen or work visa goal: 58 overall / 59 speaking
- Healthcare professional preparation: use 58 overall / 59 speaking as the working standard

For nurses and healthcare professionals, the platform should usually work with the higher healthcare-related standard unless the teacher selects another goal.

---

## Feedback Style

Feedback should feel like a real teacher wrote it.

Use natural, clear, supportive language.

Avoid robotic phrases like:

- The student demonstrated competency in...
- The learner exhibited adequate performance...
- This area requires improvement in order to optimize outcomes...

Prefer language like:

- You handled the topic well today.
- Your answer was clearer when you gave a specific example.
- The next thing to work on is organizing your answer before you speak.
- This will help your response sound more complete and easier to follow.

Feedback should be warm, but still direct.

Do not exaggerate praise. Be honest and useful.

## MET Vocabulary Support

The platform should include recurring support for high-frequency MET vocabulary.

Vocabulary should be organized by topic, level, and function.

Common topic areas include:

- education and learning
- work and career
- healthcare and nursing
- technology and online learning
- community and city life
- environment
- personal decisions and lifestyle
- communication
- public services
- health and wellbeing

Vocabulary resources should include:

- word lists
- definitions
- example sentences
- collocations
- phrasal verbs when useful
- speaking prompts
- writing prompts
- short quizzes
- review tasks

Vocabulary should not be presented as isolated word memorization only. It should help students actually speak, write, and understand MET-style topics.

---

## Healthcare English

Healthcare communication should be practical and realistic.

Include scenarios such as:

- patient handover
- explaining medication
- describing symptoms
- giving instructions
- reassuring a worried patient
- asking clarification questions
- explaining procedures
- reporting changes in condition
- communicating with coworkers
- handling difficult patient situations

The tone should be professional, calm, and clear.

Healthcare English should help students sound safe, respectful, and understandable.

---

## Creative Class Design

The platform should support creative but structured teaching.

Creative activities should still connect to:

- the student's weakness
- the MET task
- the target vocabulary or grammar
- the student's real-life communication needs

Good class formats include:

- before/after speaking tasks
- role card challenges
- patient roleplays
- vocabulary-to-performance activities
- error correction tasks
- listening detective activities
- timed answer upgrades
- model answer analysis
- guided practice before independent production

Creativity should not mean random games. It should mean useful variation with a clear learning purpose.

---

## Content Creation Rules

When creating materials, prefer reusable formats.

Materials may need to become:

- platform lessons
- student resources
- teacher notes
- homework
- Markdown files
- PDFs
- JSON objects
- dashboard cards
- resource library items
- task bank entries

Whenever possible, structure content clearly so it can be reused later.

A good task should include:

- title
- skill
- level
- MET connection
- topic
- instructions
- task
- answer key or rubric
- vocabulary focus
- teacher notes
- follow-up activity

- practice studio
- resource library
- vocabulary bank

---

## Announcements / Board

The announcement board should feel human and useful, not corporate or robotic.

It should be used for:

- class reminders
- platform updates
- study tips
- important deadlines
- new resources
- general encouragement
- MET preparation notices

Announcement tone should be clear, direct, and friendly.

Example tone:

> "New speaking practice is available this week. Focus on giving fuller answers with examples. Try recording your response before class so we can review it together."

Avoid tone like:

> "Users are hereby notified that additional platform resources have been deployed."

---

## Writing Style for the Platform

Use clear English.

Prefer short paragraphs.

Avoid unnecessary jargon.

Avoid overly formal academic language in student-facing areas.

The platform should sound professional, but still human.

Good tone: calm, clear, encouraging, practical, direct, teacher-like.

Bad tone: robotic, cold, vague, childish, too motivational, too corporate.

---

## Development Priorities

When making changes, prioritize:

1. Clarity for the student
2. Usefulness for the teacher
3. Clean visual design
4. Reusable learning content
5. Accurate diagnosis
6. Teacher review before publishing
7. Simple workflows before complex features

Do not overbuild unnecessary features.

A simple feature that helps the student study is better than a complex feature that looks impressive but does not improve learning.

---

## The Standard

This platform should feel like a serious teacher built a clear path for the student.

It should not feel like a generic AI tool.

The student should feel: *"I know what I need to do next."*

The teacher should feel: *"This saves me time without reducing my quality."*

That is the standard.
